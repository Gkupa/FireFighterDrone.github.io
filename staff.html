Tsotne Mtchedlishvili

Tsotne Mtchedlishvili is one of the computer engineers, primary design artists, lead web developer. While contributing greatly to the visual and the web development side of the project, he also parttook in assembling the drone as well as developing the custom 3D pritnted assets for the aircraft. Assembling a drone for a final project was a fascinating experience that tested my skills in various areas. From designing and taking precise measurements for the lower part of the drone to building and publishing our main website, I had the opportunity to exercise my creativity, technical expertise, and teamwork abilities. In addition to contributing to the drone's physical construction, I also played a crucial role in mounting the remote transmitter, ensuring that the drone could be controlled with precision and ease. Building a 3D model of the lower part of the drone for our university final project was no easy feat. The task required meticulous precision in pinpointing the exact locations for each screw, without the benefit of any pre-existing measurements for this unique craft. To complicate matters further, our team also determined that the drone camera needed to be mounted on the floor, requiring me to create additional space for a servo engine. With no space on the default frame for the servo to tilt the camera accordingly, I had to use my creative problem-solving skills to devise a solution. One of the biggest challenges was building a conjunction to hold the dropping mechanism, which needed to be both reliable and solid to withstand the weight and pressure it would encounter during operation. We had to ensure that the mechanism was not only secure but also easy to use, adding to the complexity of the project. Despite these challenges, I found the entire experience exhilarating, and I am proud to have contributed to a project that displays innovation, teamwork, and technical expertise. I created 3D model with is shown in report paragraph 3D printed frame by clicking here you will be able to see the result. One of the most exciting tasks in building our university final project was mounting the remote transmitter 'RADIOMASTER Receiver R81' to our flight controller. The transmitter had a range of over 1km, which was more than sufficient for our project needs. However, the process of mounting and configuring the transmitter was not without its challenges. I had to carefully connect the special pins RX1, 5V, and GND to the flight controller to ensure a reliable connection. After mounting the transmitter, the next step was to configure the remote controller. This involved following a bind method that required pressing and holding a bind button for three seconds. We also needed to select the D8 protocol on our remote control, and ensure that the receiver's LED flashed a red light to confirm successful binding. Finally, we had to connect the transmitter to Beta flight configuration, making sure that the receiver was on a serial-based connection and that the serial receiver was on SBUS. The process of mounting and configuring the remote transmitter was a critical step in ensuring the success of our project. We took great care to document each step, including screenshots for clarity, in our report. Giorgi Kupatadze

Throughout the course of designing a Firefighter Drone with the ability to monitor and extinguish small local fires, my role entailed identifying the optimal frame, installing the FC onto the drone, attaching the servo engines, expertly configuring them through beta flight configuration and helping with creating our website. Such responsibilities were crucial in ensuring the successful development and deployment of the Firefighter Drone, which ultimately serves as a cutting-edge tool in firefighting efforts. When designing our drone, we were faced with the challenge of finding the most suitable frame to meet our ambitious goals. After extensive research and evaluation, we determined that the 7-inch Apex/Mark 7 drone frame was the optimal choice. This carbon fiber frame boasts exceptional strength and durability, making it the ideal solution for our project, as we were seeking to lift as much weight as possible within a tight budget. In my role on the team, I was tasked with properly assembling the various components of the frame, which included implementing changes to the standard assembly process to accommodate the custom 3D printed parts that were developed by our talented team member, Tsotne Mchedlishvili. These modifications were crucial to the overall success of our project, as they allowed us to fully integrate the 3D printed parts into the drone's design, enabling us to achieve our desired performance and functionality. As I delved into the technical aspects of our university final project, I eagerly took on the challenge of mounting key components onto the frame. My next set of duties revolved around the installation of parts onto the motherboard Fc. Specifically, my focus was on mounting two servo engines that could be remotely controlled. Despite encountering some initial setbacks with the typical instructions not having pre-made pins for our servo engines, I remained determined to find a solution that would allow us to meet our project's requirements. Using the Diatone Mamba F722APP Stack FPV FC and ESC Flight controller, I carefully identified the pins that would enable us to achieve our desired functionality. The first servo engine was mounted on a LED pin, which had its own timer and was the most reliable option available. However, the second engine presented a new challenge, as there were no available LED pins. Undeterred, I continued my search for an alternative pin that also had its own timer. Eventually, I discovered that our controller supported the PPN pin, which I successfully mounted the second engine. Throughout the installation process, I also made use of the controller's various voltage and ground pins to ensure that the servo engines were receiving the proper power supply. Overall, this experience highlighted the importance of creative problem solving and resourcefulness in the face of technical challenges. For our university final project, the next step involved configuring the flight controller to enable the precise and smooth control of our drone's movements. Using the powerful Beta flight configuration software, I was able to make several crucial changes to our flight controller. One of the most important changes I implemented was enabling servo tilt, a function that allows us to stabilize the gimbal on both pitch and roll axes. This feature proved to be essential to our project, as it enabled us to precisely control the camera and drop objects from the drone with ease. To achieve this, I utilized the Command Line Interface (CLI) feature of the Beta flight configuration software. By inputting specific commands, I was able to customize the default pins of the flight controller to align with our project's unique needs. This step is explained in our report in Drone Configuration part. Overall, this experience further emphasized the importance of understanding and utilizing advanced software tools in the field of drone design and engineering. By effectively leveraging the power of Beta flight configuration, we were able to customize our drone's functionality to meet our project's ambitious goals.
